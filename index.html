Psychological Research Tool

For decades, psychology did basically one thing: try to fix super sick and traumatized people. Then, in at the turn of the second millennia, something incredible happened.

Psychologist Martin Siligmen realized many of the negative behaviours he studied were learned, and asked himself: Could psychology help people learn to thrive, as well?

What happens next is the birth of Positive Psychology, a field where astounding insight has emerged on mental health, wellness, and happiness. Yet, very few people know of the field, let alone navigate it.

I worked with mental health professionals, researchers, and educators to create a tool which allows practitioners to understand the growth and connection between the 19 most influential researchers in the field.

I used web-scraping, SemanticScholar's API, and D3 to create this early prototype. I'm currently collaborating with psychologists at Dartmouth to expand the tool to millions of scientific papers using neo4j.
