Psychological Research Tool

Post WWII, something unprecendent happened in psychology: an entire field new field emerged, focused on promoting human thriving, rather than just treating psychological disfunction.

Over the decades sense, astounding insight has emerged from this field of "positive psychology." Yet, very few people know or can access it.

I worked with mental health professionals, researchers, and educators to create a tool which allows practitioners to understand the growth and connection between the 19 most influential researchers in the field.

I used web-scraping, SemanticScholar's API, and D3 to create this early prototype.

To take it to the next level, I'm currently collaborating with psychologists at Dartmouth to expand the tool to millions of scientific papers, build on neo4j's graph optimizations.
